The Foundation

Job 38:36 — "Who has put wisdom in the inward parts? Or who has given understanding to the mind?"
Psalm 36:9 — "For with You is the fountain of life; in Your light shall we see light."
Hebrews 9:27 — "And just as it is appointed for men to die once, and after that comes judgment."
Proverbs 2:6 — "For it is the Lord who gives wisdom; knowledge and understanding. Out of His mouth come knowledge and understanding."

This scroll declares: Salvation is inward — a piercing of the heart, not a performance of the soul. Only the Spirit reveals truth, breaks deception, and opens the door of the spiritual heart.

The Great Deception

The Holy Spirit reveals that Satan's deepest attack is in the arena of salvation — the planting of tares amongst the wheat. The Psalms below open a prophetic window into this assault: Psalm 4 (wake-up call), Psalm 62 (posture of silence and trust), and Psalm 30 (transformation from deception to joy).

Soul

Often deceived; cries out for God.

Spirit

Lured by vanity and self-righteousness.

Body

Can be religious in mechanics but barren in heart.

Self-righteousness is the snake of the garden. Only the Spirit can open the heart in true repentance. A death to self must occur before one is truly born again.

Psalm 4 — The Divine Interruption

Psalm 4:2 — God cries: "How long will you turn my glory into shame? How long will you love vanity? How long will you seek deception forever?"

These three questions map the three-part fall of man:

  • Turn my glory into shame? — Soul
  • Love vanity? — Spirit
  • Seek deception forever? — Body into eternity

Psalm 4:3 — "But know that the Lord has set apart for Himself him that is wonderful." This points to Jesus (see Isaiah 9:6): Wonderful — Body; Counsellor — Soul; Mighty God — Spirit.

Job's parallel: Job's righteousness required the stripping of self-righteousness. Only after sackcloth and repentance did God become one with him. Salvation demands a death to self.

Three Commands (Psalm 4:4–5)

  1. Be angry and sin not. Acknowledge deception.
  2. Commune with your heart and be still. Separate and search your heart before God.
  3. Offer the sacrifices of righteousness and trust in the Lord. This is the death of self and the birth of new life.

Transformation — Psalm 30 & Psalm 4

Psalm 4:7 — "You have put gladness in my heart, more than in the time that their wheat and their wine and their oil increased." Wine (Soul), Wheat (Body), Oil (Spirit).

Psalm 30:11–12 — "You have turned my mourning into joy; you have put off my sackcloth and girded me with gladness; therefore I will give thanks forever." This is the garment-change of salvation: mourning → joy; sackcloth → gladness; silence → eternal praise.

The end of Psalm 4:8 — "In peace I will both lie down and sleep; for you alone make me dwell in safety" — is the eternal seal. The cycle completes: death → surrender → union → peace → light.

Closing: Salvation is not a program or a feeling. It is a spiritual crucible where self is consumed and the Spirit births a Royal Priest before God. The heart that has been truly pierced will offer genuine sacrifices of praise, and the Lord will turn His face in love and answer.
